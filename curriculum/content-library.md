## Skill 1: Context Assembly
*Knowing what information to provide AI and why*

### Videos

- **[The ULTIMATE Prompt Engineering Course](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jC4v5AS4RIM)** - YouTube (Matt Wolfe) - 32 min
  - Why it's good: Comprehensive walkthrough of what information to include in prompts. Covers role-setting, examples, and constraints. Beginner-friendly with practical examples.

- **[Prompt Engineering Tutorial – Master ChatGPT and LLM Responses](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_ZvnD96BsTs)** - YouTube (freeCodeCamp) - 2 hours (watch first 30 min for context basics)
  - Why it's good: Structured curriculum approach. First section covers exactly what context elements matter and why.

- **[How to Use ChatGPT to Write Better Prompts](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jHv63Uvk5VA)** - YouTube (Jeff Su) - 8 min
  - Why it's good: Short, practical. Shows the "meta-prompt" technique—asking AI to help you assemble better context.

- **[OpenAI Prompt Engineering Guide Explained](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=aOKB_Kx3R40)** - YouTube (AI Explained) - 18 min
  - Why it's good: Breaks down OpenAI's official best practices into actionable steps. Explains the "why" behind each context element.

- **[Claude's System Prompts: What They Teach Us](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qF9_Xk6xmg4)** - YouTube (AI Explained) - 15 min
  - Why it's good: Shows how professionals structure context for AI assistants. Transferable principles for any AI interaction.

### Articles

- **[Prompt Engineering Guide - OpenAI Platform](https://platform.openai.com/docs/guides/prompt-engineering)** - Official Documentation - 15 min read
  - Why it's good: The source. Six strategies straight from OpenAI: write clear instructions, provide reference text, split complex tasks, give the model time to think, use external tools, test changes systematically.

- **[Prompt Engineering Guide](https://www.promptingguide.ai/)** - DAIR.AI - Reference site
  - Why it's good: Comprehensive, free, and regularly updated. Covers context assembly patterns with examples across different use cases.

- **[Working with AI: Two Paths to Prompting](https://www.oneusefulthing.org/p/working-with-ai-two-paths-to-prompting)** - Ethan Mollick (One Useful Thing) - 8 min read
  - Why it's good: Distinguishes between "structured prompting" and "conversational prompting." Helps users understand when each context approach works best.

- **[How I Use AI](https://simonwillison.net/2024/Dec/17/how-i-use-ai/)** - Simon Willison - 12 min read
  - Why it's good: Real-world examples from a developer who uses AI daily. Shows exactly what context he provides and why.

- **[The Art of ChatGPT Prompting](https://fka.gumroad.com/l/art-of-chatgpt-prompting)** - Free ebook - 45 min read
  - Why it's good: Systematic framework for constructing prompts. Good for people who want a repeatable process.

### Tutorials

- **[Google's Prompt Design Strategies](https://cloud.google.com/vertex-ai/docs/generative-ai/learn/prompt-design)** - Google Cloud - Interactive guide
  - Why it's good: Enterprise-focused. Shows how to provide context for business use cases.

## Skill 2: Quality Judgment

### Videos

- **[AI Hallucinations Explained](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Rf5XaHvmSp4)** - YouTube (IBM Technology) - 7 min
  - Why it's good: Clear explanation of why AI makes confident mistakes. Essential for understanding when to verify.

- **[Can You Trust AI? How to Spot Hallucinations](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=CXhprEnXQRc)** - YouTube (Fireship) - 5 min
  - Why it's good: Fast, funny, accurate. Shows real examples of AI confidently being wrong.

- **[GPT-4 vs Claude vs Gemini: Which AI Should You Trust?](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=UnK5gVy0Qlk)** - YouTube (Matt Wolfe) - 20 min
  - Why it's good: Comparative analysis helps users understand different models' strengths and weaknesses.

- **[How to Fact-Check AI](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=FZhbJZEgKQ4)** - YouTube (Vox) - 10 min
  - Why it's good: Journalist perspective on verification. Transferable to any professional context.

- **[When ChatGPT Gets It Wrong](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kYqRtjDBci8)** - YouTube (Two Minute Papers) - 6 min
  - Why it's good: Academic rigor applied to AI errors. Shows systematic ways AI fails.

### Articles

- **[What AI Can Do and What It Can't](https://www.oneusefulthing.org/p/what-ai-can-do-and-what-it-cant)** - Ethan Mollick - 10 min read
  - Why it's good: Written by a Wharton professor who tests AI extensively. Evidence-based assessment of capabilities.

- **[Large Language Models and the Reversal Curse](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/HfAvLdRhGzc3ynMsD/large-language-models-and-the-reversal-curse)** - LessWrong - 15 min read
  - Why it's good: Deep dive into a specific AI limitation. Helps users understand the nature of AI knowledge gaps.

- **[AI Detectors Are Not Reliable](https://theconversation.com/ai-detectors-have-high-error-rates-especially-for-non-native-english-writers-206933)** - The Conversation - 6 min read

- **[My AI Told Me To](https://www.oneusefulthing.org/p/my-ai-told-me-to)** - Ethan Mollick - 8 min read
  - Why it's good: Examines the psychology of trusting AI. Helps users develop appropriate skepticism.

- **[What I Think About LLMs](https://simonwillison.net/2024/Apr/7/what-i-think-about-llms/)** - Simon Willison - 10 min read
  - Why it's good: Shows how AI reliability varies by task type. More abstract = more risk.

- **[When Should You Trust AI Output?](https://newsletter.superhuman.ai/p/when-should-you-trust-ai-output)** - Superhuman AI Newsletter - 5 min read
  - Why it's good: Quick decision framework. Practical checklist approach.

### Tutorials

- **[Fact-Checking AI with AI](https://www.promptingguide.ai/techniques/verification)** - DAIR.AI - Hands-on guide
  - Why it's good: Shows techniques for using AI to verify AI output. Practical verification workflows.
